Introduction

Understanding Large Number Systems

The world uses two primary large number systems: the International System (Thousand → Million → Billion → Trillion) and the Indian System (Hazar → Lakh → Crore). These systems diverge significantly above 100,000, making billion to crore conversion essential for finance professionals, students, and business analysts working across global markets.


Whether you're reading a global financial report in Billions or an Indian stock market report in Crores, our converter bridges the gap instantly. The core formula is simple: 1 Billion = 100 Crore, yet the broader number system differences run deep — affecting how we read corporate revenues, national budgets, and investment values.

How It Works

The Conversion Formula

Simple math, clear results. Here's the step-by-step breakdown of how large number conversion works between international and Indian systems.

01
Identify Your Input Unit
Choose whether you're working with Billions, Millions, Trillions (international) or Crores, Lakhs, Thousands (Indian system). This determines the base multiplier.
02
Apply the Multiplier
Each unit has a fixed numeric value. For example, 1 Billion = 1,000,000,000 (10⁹). Multiply your input by this base value to get the raw number.
03
Divide by Target Unit
Divide the raw number by the target unit's value. 1 Crore = 10,000,000 (10⁷), so 1 Billion ÷ 10⁷ = 100 Crore. The result is your converted value.
04
Format and Verify
Apply proper Indian or International number formatting. 100 Crore becomes 1,00,00,00,000 in Indian format — same number, different presentation.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about billion to crore conversion and large number systems.

How many crores is 1 billion? +
1 Billion equals exactly 100 Crore. This is because 1 Billion = 1,000,000,000 and 1 Crore = 10,000,000. So 1,000,000,000 ÷ 10,000,000 = 100 Crore. This is the most common conversion needed in Indian financial contexts.
How many millions is 1 crore? +
1 Crore equals 10 Million. Since 1 Crore = 1,00,00,000 in the Indian system and 1 Million = 10,00,000, dividing gives us 10 Million per Crore.
What is the difference between Billion and Arab? +
In the Indian numbering system, 100 Crore is called "Arab." This is exactly equal to 1 Billion in the international system. So 1 Billion = 1 Arab = 100 Crore.
How many lakhs is 1 billion? +
1 Billion = 10,000 Lakh. Since 1 Lakh = 1,00,000 and 1 Billion = 1,00,00,00,000, dividing gives 10,000 Lakh. So if a company earns ₹1 Billion, it earned ₹10,000 Lakh.
Why do India and Western countries use different number systems? +
The Indian numbering system evolved separately and groups digits differently after the first three digits. After 999, the Indian system uses groups of two (Lakh = 1,00,000; Crore = 1,00,00,000), while the international system always groups in threes (Million = 1,000,000; Billion = 1,000,000,000).
How do I convert 5.7 Billion to Crore? +
Multiply 5.7 × 100 = 570 Crore. The formula is: Value in Crore = Value in Billion × 100. So 5.7 Billion = 570 Crore = 5,70,00,00,000. You can also use our converter above for instant results.
Is 1 trillion equal to how many crores? +
1 Trillion = 1,00,000 Crore (One Lakh Crore). Since 1 Trillion = 1,000 Billion and 1 Billion = 100 Crore, multiplying gives 1,000 × 100 = 1,00,000 Crore. The Indian government often refers to large budgets in "Lakh Crore."
Does the converter support decimal values? +
Yes! Our converter fully supports decimal inputs. For example, entering 2.5 Billion will give you 250 Crore. You can enter any decimal number and get precise results up to 6 decimal places in the output.
